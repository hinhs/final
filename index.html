What is an amino acid?

Like the 26 letters of the Alphabet form all the words, the common 20 amino acids form protein in biology. Just a chain of these simple molcular structure create everything.

All amino acids have the same basic structure. They contain three groups: carboxlylic acid, amine, and a side chain. It's almost like a super amphoteric versitile structure. What separates all these amino acids from one another is the side chain group. The differering strucutres have both varying chemical and physical properties like big or small groups, acidic or basic, and polar and non-polar.
